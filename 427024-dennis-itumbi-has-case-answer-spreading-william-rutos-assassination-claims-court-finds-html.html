Dennis Itumbi Has Case to Answer for Spreading William Ruto's Assassination Claims, Court Finds

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Friday, March 15, 2024
  • Milimani chief magistrate Martha Mutuku on Wednesday, September 22, ruled that the two will have to defend themselves over the accusations
  • Dennis Itumbi and his co-accused blogger Samwel Gateri Wanjiru are charged with publishing a falsified letter with the intent to cause anxiety to the public
  • In the said letter, the accused persons allegedly claimed that powerful government officers met at Hotel La Mada in Nairobi and planned to assassinate Ruto

Milimani Court has ruled out that former state house digital director Dennis Itumbi and his co-accused blogger Samwel Gateri Wanjiru have a case to answer in the fake Deputy President William Ruto's assassination letter.

Milimani chief magistrate Martha Mutuku on Wednesday, September 22, said the two will have to defend themselves.

The two are charged with publishing a falsified letter dated May 30, 2018, with the intent to cause anxiety to the general public contrary to Section 66(1) of the Penal Code.

La Mada Hotel meeting

Appearing before Mutuku in August, Itumbi's lawyer Katwa Kigen pleaded with the court to dismiss the case arguing that it lacks merit.

The case will be mentioned on September 23, to fix the hearing date for their defence.

Fake Ruto assassination letter

TUKO.co.ke had earlier on reported that the hearing of a case against Itumbi and Gateri over a fake Ruto's assassination plot had commenced.

While testifying before the court, Sergent Isaac Ruto said the alarming message which was circulated on social media was sent from Gateri's cell phone number.

He added that the blogger later pulled down the post after realising that it had caused anxiety.

The court heard that Itumbi circulated the letter in a WhatsApp group allied to the second in command.

The purported letter indicated a section of leaders from the Mount Kenya region were determined to kill the DP before the 2022 General Election.

The letter alleged a meeting was to plan how to stop Ruto from ascending to power in 2022.

The meeting was reportedly attended by CSs James Macharia (Transport), Joseph Mucheru (ICT), Sicily Kariuki (Health) and Peter Munya (Trade).

Although the CSs confirmed they attended the meeting, Munya, on behalf of his colleagues, vehemently denied the allegations against them.
