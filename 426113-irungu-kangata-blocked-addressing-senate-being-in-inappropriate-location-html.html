Irungu Kang'ata Blocked from Addressing Senate for Being In 'Inappropriate' Location

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Sunday, February 4, 2024
  • Murang'a senator Irungu Kang'ata could not address the ongoing session in Senate after he was spotted in a wrong location
  • The senator insisted he was at his office but still could not be allowed to contribute
  • Speaker Kenneth Lusaka turned him down and demanded he finds an appropriate place before he could be allowed to address the Senate

On Tuesday, the Senate was treated to a light moment after Murang'a senator Irungu Kang'ata was denied opportunity take part in an ongoing session for allegedly being in a location deemed inappropriate.

The upper house was deliberating on the alleged discriminative enforcement of the nationwide curfew for containment of the COVID-19 pandemic, a debate Kang'ata logged in to contribute to.

However, the first-term senator who was at the time away from the Senate buildings could not be spotted despite his voice being clear.

Speaker Kenneth Lusaka insisted that the senator turns on the video on his device.

The speaker later noticed that Kang'ata was not in the right location to give his two cents in the ongoing debate.

He was turned down until he finds an appropriate place from where he would contribute.

"...We can't see you, we are not seeing you. Where are you. That is not an office. I am sorry we can't allow that. The rules are very clear on where you should be. So you are out of order senator. When you are in an appropriate place I will give you an opportunity," said Lusaka who was visibly perplexed upon spotting Kanga'ta's live location.

In a separate story, Soy MP Caleb Kosittany caused a stir in parliament in June after he showed up while clad in colours deemed to reflect the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party.

The lawmaker had donned a yellow mask and a yellow pullover covered by a black coat.

Rising on a point of order, Homa Bay MP Gladys Wanga questioned whether it was appropriate for Kosittany to dress in the presumed UDA colours while on the floor of the house.

Wanga was seconded by Navakholo MP Emmanuel Wangwe, and Suba's John Mbadi who recalled the harassment meted on ODM legislators when they dressed up in orange colours while attending parliament proceedings.

"I'm concerned because this house must not allow discrimination. I remember sometimes back when I came to the parliament precincts in ODM colours and the security refused that I should not come in," said Mbadi.

The Suba MP requested the speaker to have Kosittany ejected from the house, and the sergeant at arms questioned for allowing the MP in despite being in the know of the standing orders touching on the parliament dressing code.

Nonetheless, the speaker brushed off the argument saying the sergeants at arm were the ones to establish whether the colours were synonymous with the DP William Ruto-linked UDA party.
