Mike Sonko to spend 2 more nights in police cell ahead of bail application ruling

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Monday, May 20, 2024

- Mike Sonko was arrested on Friday, December 6, following and order by Director of Public Prosecutions

- The Nairobi governor denied numerous counts of corruption in company of his lawyers and pleaded for reasonable bail terms

- The prosecution asked the court to deny Sonko bail on grounds that he was a flight risk

- Sonko's lawyers also requested for their client to be released on bail to get medical attention

- Magistrate Douglas Ogoti scheduled the bail ruling on Wednesday, December 11

Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko will be forced to cool his heels behind bars until Wednesday, December 11, when his bail application will be heard.

The gold-loving governor was arrested on Friday, December 6, following an order from Director of Public Prosecution (DPP) Noordin Haji and denied over 10 counts of money laundering and other economic crimes before a Nairobi court on Monday, December 9.

Delivering the ruling in a jam packed court dominated with murmurs at the back of passionate submission from a number of Sonko's 12 lawyers, Chief Magistrate Douglas Ogoti said he would rule on the matter on Wednesday, December 11.

"Having heard all parties on all issues, I hereby issue orders to the Investigating Officer for summons of accused persons who did not attend today at the next mention. The accused will remain remanded in custody. Given the urgent need for medical attention, though disputed, I order he be escorted to any other hospital for attention as soon as that can be done after assessment by the prison's health officer," ruled Ogoti.

The prosecution unrelentingly argued against administration of bailout terms for Sonko terming him a flight risk while the embattled governors' lawyers in one tune pleaded with the court to offer reasonable terms to their client.

Sonko's lawyer, Cecil Miller Jr, citing a Dr Esther Nafula's medical report, said his client needed to be released immediately because he had developed chest complications and suffered a fractured rib during his dramatic arrest in Voi.

As prosecution and defence lawyers did their best to outdo each other during the bail hearing, Sonko's wife Primrose Mbuvi sat quietly in the courtroom, occasionally sinking in a trance, hoping the court's decision would favour her husband.

She, however, had to leave with mixed feelings as the court ordered the police to detain Sonko in police custody pending a ruling on bail application and at the same time ordered that the governor be taken to hospital.
