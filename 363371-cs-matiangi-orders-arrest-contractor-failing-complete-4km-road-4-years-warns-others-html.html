CS Matiang'i orders arrest of contractor for failing to complete 4km road in 4 years, warns others

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Monday, July 29, 2024

- Interior CS Fred Matiang'i issued a warning to contractors who like playing games with public resources

- He said some were delaying in delivering their projects even after being given resources to complete them

- The CS said a contractor in Nyandarua county had delayed completing a four-kilometre road four years down the line

- Matiang'i instructed police to arrest and charge him for misusing public resources

Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i has ordered for the immediate arrest of a contractor who failed to complete a four and a half kilometre road for over four years after he was awarded the tender.

Speaking on Thursday, June 18, after meeting with a delegation of Western Kenya leaders, the tough-talking CS said he had ordered police in Nyandarua county to track down the contractor and have him arrested.

Matiang'i said his tours in counties had revealed a loophole in the implementation of government projects as exhibited by contractors who like playing cat and mouse games with public resources.

“We have been moving around and come across cases where contractors are basically just playing games or some of them are just coming from that old school [of thought] where they believed in navigating their way around by bribing public officials,” he said.

The CS said the matter was brought to his attention during his recent visit to Nyandarua county where he toured to assess development projects that had been launched by the Jubilee government.

“I was in Nyandarua on Monday where a contractor took up an assignment to do four and a half kilometres of a road about four years ago. "I asked the police team to look for the contractor and arrest him so that we actually can take them to court for wasting public resources. We’re not going to play those kinds of games anymore,” he said.

At the same time, the CS sounded a warning to grassroots project supervisors saying they risked losing their jobs for failing to ensure projects were completed in a timely manner.

“We must love our country enough to do the right thing and do the work that we have been assigned to do. We are employed by the people of Kenya to get this work done; we cannot lie around pretending and wasting public resources,” he said.

The CS took issue with the Lake Victoria North Water Company which he said had claimed to have done so many borehole projects in Western Kenya but none of them was complete.

In 2019, President Uhuru Kenyatta appointed CS Matiang'i to chair the National Development Implementation and Communication Committee which supervises all government projects.
