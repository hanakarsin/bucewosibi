Joshua Kutuny warns more Jubilee rebels will be expelled from party: &quot;Lazima kuwe na heshima&quot;

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Monday, April 15, 2024

- Joshua Kutuny said he would push for democracy in Jubilee Party but cautioned members against disrespecting party leader, President Uhuru Kenyatta

- The Cherangany lawmaker promised to kick out disrespectful members and restore sanity within the ruling outfit

- The vocal legislator who is a pro-handshake supporter said he won't hesitate to support a motion to impeach Deputy President William Ruto for allegedly sabotaging government's agenda

Newly appointed Jubilee deputy secretary-general Joshua Kutuny has vowed to straighten things in the ruling outfit and has sent a warning to perceived rebels.

The Cherangany Member of Parliament said he will push for sanity and democracy in the party but cautioned members against disrespecting party leader Uhuru Kenyatta and Jubilee's top hierarchy.

Speaking at St Peters Nyakinywa Secondary school in his constituency where he donated a bus to the institution, the lawmaker promised to kick out Jubilee members whom he said were not giving Uhuru the respect he deserves as the symbol of unity.

"You cannot continue serving in government while at the same time criticising it yet it pays your salary. You also cannot stay in a party that sponsored you to Parliament if you at the same time want to bring it down...lazima kuwe na heshima (there must be respect)""If you have any problems with the party, democracy dictates that you can resign and seek fresh mandate from the people and leave the rest to continue implementing its policies," Kutuny said.

The vocal Cherangany legislator who is a pro-handshake supporter said he won't hesitate to support a motion to impeach Ruto for sabotaging the Jubilee government's agenda.

"I am not aware of any disciplinary action against Ruto though I am in agreement with those who are saying that we need to crack the whip on these people who have been deliberately undermining the party leader’s efforts," he said.
