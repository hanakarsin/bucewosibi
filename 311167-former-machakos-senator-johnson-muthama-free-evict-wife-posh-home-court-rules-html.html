Former Machakos senator Johnson Muthama free to evict ex-wife from posh home, court rules

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Tuesday, May 28, 2024

- Muthama married Agnes Kavindu in 1975 and they sired three children before their separation in 1983

- The separation was through a court process but they later reunited

- Kavindu made an application seeking injunction orders against sale of their home in Mua

- Despite their reunion, the court held that the two remained separated and were only cohabiting

Former senator Johnson Muthama has won a case against his ex-wife Agnes Kavindu in a dispute over his posh home in Machakos county.

The Court of Appeal Judge J. N. Onyiego dismissed an appeal by Kavindu after High Court Judge William Musyoka allowed Muthama to evict her from the home.

She appealed the ruling on March 14, 2019 seeking not to be evicted from her Mua house.

The mother of three applied for an injunction restraining any sale, disposal, alienation or any dealings with the properties listed as matrimonial property.

Onyiego maintained that their divorce in court in 1983 still stands despite having reunited and cohabited for some time.

He dismissed the case with no order on costs saying reasons stated in the application were not merited.

"Concerning whether the matter is jurisdicata and that the court is functus officio, I hold that I have not been invited to determine the suit nor the applications determined by my colleague,The same cannot amount to jurisdicata nor is this court functus officio. In the same vein, I am not in any way sitting in an appellate capacity on the decision of my brother," read part of the ruling.

The two married in 1975 and sired three children who are now adults before they separated through a court process in 1983.

The row over the property started way back in 2014 when Muthama wanted to dispose the property.

Kavindu moved to court and obtained injunction orders against any sale of the home.
