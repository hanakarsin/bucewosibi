Egg curry recipe Kenya style: how to prepare

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Sunday, March 24, 2024

Eggs are one of the most versatile ingredients in your kitchen. In fact, most people who don’t eat red meat opt for eggs. In only 25 minutes you will be able to prepare delicious curry, making it the easiest curry ingredient which is also everyone’s favorite. Both meat lovers and eggetarian vegetarians adore this tasty meal. Do you know how to make egg curry?

If you remain with leftover eggs that had been served in your party, it is still possible for you to prepare curry with the leftovers so you can enjoy eating a quick rice and eggs meal. Notably, kids just like adults enjoy the taste of eggs, and their parents prefer the meal due to its health benefits.

Egg curry ingredients

For a spicy egg curry recipe, you need to have the following ingredients.

  • Two eggs. Ensure that they are boiled and cut into halves or quarters.
  • Two finely chopped onions
  • Two finely chopped tomatoes
  • Two finely chopped cloves garlic
  • Finely chopped ginger
  • One spring curry leaf
  • ¾ teaspoon of Garam masala
  • ¼ teaspoon of coriander
  • ¼ teaspoon of black pepper powder
  • ¼ teaspoon of turmeric (Haldi)
  • One stalk of finely chopped Dhania (coriander leaves)
  • ¼ cup of water
  • Two tablespoons of cooking oil
  • Salt

Egg curry process

Below is the procedure of egg curry recipe Kerala style.

  • Begin by heating the cooking oil (or coconut oil) in a pan and add chopped onions, ginger, curry leaves and garlic. Lower the heat to medium level then saute until your onions become tender and golden brown.

    2. After the onions are well browned, add your gram masala, chili, turmeric, coriander, and pepper powders then saute until the condiments produce a roasted aroma.

    3. Proceed to add your chopped tomato and salt to taste. Simmer the mixture for several minutes till the tomatoes begin to boil.

    4. At this level, you can add the ¼ cup of water and let the mixture boil. When it finally boils, you should turn the heat to low, add your boiled eggs and then cover and simmer your eggs for 10 – 15 minutes. You will then switch off the heat, add your chopped coriander leaves before stirring the mixture gently so that your eggs don’t crumble.

    Serve the egg curry gravy with steamed rice, ugali, appam or even chapatti.
