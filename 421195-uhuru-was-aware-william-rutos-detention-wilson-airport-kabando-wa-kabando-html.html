Uhuru Was Aware of William Ruto's Detention at Wilson Airport, Kabando Wa Kabando

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Friday, August 2, 2024
  • Kabando's sentiments came at the back of a recent injunction that barred Ruto and his seven-man entourage from travelling to Uganda
  • The second in command was blocked after it emerged that he allegedly did not seek clearance as all civil servants should
  • The name of a Turkish national identified as Harun Aydin emerged hours after the trip was aborted

Former Mukurweini lawmaker Kabando Wa Kabando has said President Uhuru Kenyatta was aware of Deputy President William Ruto's detention at Nairobi's Wilson Airport.

Kabando's sentiments came at the back of a recent injunction that barred Ruto and his seven-man entourage from travelling to Uganda after immigration officials failed to give him clearance at the airport.

Uhuru knows everything that happens in the country

According to Kabando, Uhuru is a very well informed person and he knows everything that happens all over the country.

''He (Uhuru) is fully abreast. It is wrong to buck-pass or blame others for Ruto's travel debacle,'' Kabando tweeted.

The vocal politician attributed the incident to the fallout between Uhuru and his deputy.

''Truth is that RAIS (Uhuru) has decided to 'DEAL' with NAIBU (Ruto). It is going to get uglier. Ruto must brace for tougher rougher times ahead,'' Kabando further said.

Kabando observed that a deputy president is not supposed to leave the country without the president's green light in an ideal situation.

''Republic's second-in-command should not travel to foreign countries minus the express authority of Number one. 'Detaining' DP for five hours means UhuRuto communication has completely broken down. That was a hard tackle,'' Kabando explained, saying that things are fast falling apart between the two leaders.

Ruto said blocking his trip was unnecessary

Speaking on Inooro FM on Wednesday, August 4, the deputy president said blocking his trip was unnecessary.

Ruto observed the move was intended to push him to bicker with some individuals in government.

"I have been a deputy president for nine years and I have travelled to many countries for both official and personal reasons and not once have I ever been required to seek permission from anyone until Monday. Which law is this that has only begun recently? This was meant to cause conflict but I have left the situation in God's hands," he explained.

The DP noted that Museveni is a close friend to President Uhuru Kenyatta and many other politicians in Kenya.

Ruto's friendship with Museveni

He indicated that it had seemingly become a problem after he (Ruto) forged a friendship with the long-serving ruler.

"I'm I not entitled to friends?" the second-in-command posed.

He also said it was wrong to label the Turkish national Harun Aydin as a terrorist due to his associations with the DP, yet he was an honest investor.

The man was also to travel with the DP to Entebbe, Uganda when their flight was cancelled.
