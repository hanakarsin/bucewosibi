Besigye arrested for illegally swearing himself in as president

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Friday, May 24, 2024

- Ugandan opposition leader Kizza Besigye was arrested after he illegally swore himself in as the country's president

- This was despite the fact that he lost the election to incumbent president elect Yoweri Museveni

Tension has been for the better part of today high in Kampala, Uganda ahead of the swearing in of president elect Yoweri Museveni tomorrow on Thursday, May 12.

Museveni’s main opponent Dr Kizza Besigye today, on Wednesday, May 11 swore himself in as the president of Uganda despite not winning the presidential seat.

Uganda’s media reported that Besigye managed to sneak through a police barrier into the city and held his ceremony attended by hundreds of supporters.

It was at this time that anti riot police were dispersing people using tear gas. Besigye was later arrested.

 Museveni’s swearing in on the other hand is set to be witnessed by a number of heads of states including those from Kenya, Tanzania and Zambia among others tomorrow on Thursday, May 12.            

Besigye has been claiming that Museveni rigged the elections and therefore, he was the rightful president of the land of matoke.

He took Museveni to the Supreme Court where he challenged the results but the courts ruled in favour of Museveni who later held a large celebration party to celebrate both wins- the presidency and the court ruling.

On the very same day of his celebration. Besigye’s political party also held a celebratory party despite losing.

They claimed they were celebrating their efforts against Museveni and to show the electorate that they would not be defeated. He also announced that he would form a parallel government to Museveni’s.

Kizza Besigye was the second runners up in the February election.
