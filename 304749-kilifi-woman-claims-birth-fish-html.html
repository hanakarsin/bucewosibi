Kilifi woman claims to have given birth to fish

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Wednesday, March 6, 2024

- The woman started bleeding which was later followed by severe stomach pains

- Sarah Mbeyu later felt a massive force in the abdomen and she pushed it out

- What came out looked like fish and she claimed it was connected to something evil

- However, Kilifi medical officer said it was a medical condition called molar pregnancy

Shariani location in Kilifi county has been left in shock after one of them allegedly gave birth to a live 'fish'.

The area residents flocked Sarah Mbeyu Jabiri's homestead in numbers to catch a glimpse of what came out of her body.

All was well for Jabiri until Thursday last week when she started bleeding which was later followed by severe stomach discomfort that felt much like the labour pains, as reported by the Standard.

“I started bleeding on Thursday and Friday last week I started feeling severe stomach pains, it was too much like labour pains. The pains ceased on Sunday but I was still bleeding until Wednesday at night when I felt some force in my abdomen and I pushed the mass out,” said Jabiri.

The mother of five children was shocked to see what had come out of her body and she called her 55-year-old mother who lives nearby.

“I was not pregnant at all. I was still with my clothes and so I rushed to pick a basin so that I can drop it there, but it was late and I released it on the ground. The creature had eyes and a fishtail. I was shocked to see it,” she said.

The family suspects someone had cast a spell on Jabiri that led to such a weird creature forming in her womb.

“We took the creature and put it in water in a bucket and it started moving swinging its tail. This is not normal at all. I am shocked by what my daughter is going through. She gave birth safely without any complications,” said Jabiri's mother.

However, Kilifi County Medical Superintendent Eddy Nzomo dismissed the claims saying the 30-year-old woman had suffered from a medical condition called molar pregnancy which is likely to happen in women above 35 years or younger than 20 years.

“This is not witchcraft but a condition in the world of science. It happens when a woman gets pregnant, then the placenta starts developing but develops complications and starts growing abnormally forming a mass in the uterus. With time, the body sees it as foreign and it removes it from the uterus,” he said.

He further said the condition usually happens among pregnant women before the 27th week of pregnancy (7 months).

Jabiri, who has since been referred to Kilifi County Hospital for more tests and cleaning of the womb, is still in denial insisting the extract resembled a type of fish called ‘totovu’ which is never eaten.
