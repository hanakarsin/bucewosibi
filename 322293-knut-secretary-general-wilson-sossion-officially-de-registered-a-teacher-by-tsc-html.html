TSC officially deregisters KNUT boss Wilson Sossion as teacher

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Sunday, June 23, 2024

- The Kenya National Union of Teachers boss Wilson Sossion was de-registered as a teacher via a gazette notice on October 29, 2019

- The ODM nominated MP was removed from the list for engaging in politics contrary to the teachers act

- The teachers' spokesperson was barred from teaching in any Kenyan school

The Teachers Service Commission (TSC) has officially removed the Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT) Secretary General Wilson Sossion from its teachers' list.

Sossion was de-registered by the teachers' employer in a gazette notice by TSC CEO Nancy Macharia dated Tuesday, October 29.

The KNUT top official was removed from the list for engaging in politics contrary to the teachers code of conduct.

Section 16 of the teachers Code of Conduct and Ethics (2015) requires a tutor to maintain political neutrality at all times.

"Pursuant to section 30 of the Teachers Service Commission Act, the TSC has removed the name of the individual named here under from the register of teachers," the gazette notice read."Further under the law, the individual cannot engage in teaching in any school," it added.

Sossion was nominated into Parliament by the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) after the 2013 General Election.

On Friday, July 26, 2019, the Employment and Labour Court Judge Nelson Abuodha allowed TSC to de-register him for his engagement in politics.

“The acceptance of the nomination to National Assembly by ODM obviously puts Sossion on a partisan political activity. It was a manifestation of an agreement to support ODM agenda both in and outside Parliament,” Justice Abuodha ruled.

At the time, Sossion remained put dismissing the move as "inconsequential", maintaining he would retain his position in the union.

The ODM nominated MP cited article 34 (A) of the Labour Relations Act saying the termination of his contract did not affect his rights as a union official.

The fiery unionist obtained temporary court orders on Wednesday, August 28, preventing union members from ousting him as the teachers' spokesperson.

He said powerful individuals within government were eager to remove him from the position and put a "puppet" as a replacement.

“I will not go anywhere, leave matters of trade union alone, we shall continue demanding teachers dues until you (Teachers Service Commission) comply with the court ruling,” he warned.

In a quick rejoinder on Friday, November 1, KNUT dismissed Sossion's de-registration as an illegality saying the matter was still active in court.

"There should be no panic over the gazetted notice by TSC to deregister our SG Wilson Sossion. The notice is illegal as the matter is active in court. It is also discriminative as there are many other union leaders who are serving as Members of Parliament," it tweeted.
