DCI should tell us what they know about lawyer Paul Gicheru's surrender to ICC, Mandago

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Monday, July 8, 2024

- Governor Jackson Mandago said DCI boss George Kinoti should be genuine in his revival of the post-election violence cases

- He said he believed the reopening of the cases was also linked to the surrender of lawyer Paul Gicheru to the ICC

- Mandago also differed with Kinoti's claims of threats issued to post-election violence victims

Uasin Gishu Governor Jackson Mandago has said he has all the reasons to believe that the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss George Kinoti knows something about the surrender of lawyer Paul Gicheru to the International Criminal Court (ICC).

Mandago, who was reacting to Kinoti's announcement of reopening 2007/2008 post-election violence cases, said there was an apparent connection between the ICC case and the reopening of the PEV cases.

Speaking at a press conference on Monday, November 24, Mandago also differed with the DCI boss' claims of threats issued to post-election violence victims or any land grabbing in his county.

"As the governor of Uasin Gishu county, this county is absolutely peaceful and as county government, we are not aware of any threats issued to anybody."I have every reason to believe there must be a link with ICC issues and even in the surrender of Gicheru; DCI should tell us what they know," the county boss said.

Mandago cast aspersions on whether Kinoti was genuinely helping the victims adding that "he is operating in an unusual environment".

Furious reactions from Ruto allies

Moments after the DCI boss said he will reopen the post-election violence cases, several William Ruto allies took to their social media platforms and said they were reading malice from the whole process.

Elgeyo Marakwet Senator Kipchumba Murkomen said the move to revive the cases was a desperate effort by the "system" to bring down the deputy president and weaken his political strength in the country.

"They want to incite violence against communities in R.Valley and especially Eldoret to weaken DP’s political strength in Mt.Kenya. They may even burn houses or kill people," he said.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, the international court in a statement noted Gicheru is suspected of committing offences against the administration of justice by corruptly influencing witnesses of the Court linked to Ruto's case.

The ICC ordered for the arrest of the lawyer five years ago alongside one Philip Kipkoech who was also suspected of tampering with witnesses.
