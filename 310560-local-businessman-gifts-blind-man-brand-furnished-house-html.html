Kind businessman gifts old blind man fully furnished house

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Tuesday, March 5, 2024

A blind man finally has a roof over his head after a kind businessman donated a fully-furnished one-room house to him.

The 75-year-old had struggled for years to get a decent house and businessman Ishwar Ramlutchman changed his life after handing over a new house to him.

According to South Africa's media reports, unlike many homesteads, the new house has a toilet and tap water inside.

Macaleni Gumede survives with an old age grant and is visually impaired.

He uses most of his pension grant to pay people who help him as he lives alone.

The new homeowner expressed joy at his new place of residence while thanking the businessman.

"If I die today, I am sure my spirit will join my ancestors, because I am now living a in a decent house," he said.

He went on to say that he experienced difficulty using an outside toilet because of his condition.

‘It is hard for me to use an outside toilet owing to my condition. I have been struggling for a long time to have a decent home. I was living a shameful life, living a shack," said the old man.

He has wished blessings on the good Samaritan who put him in a proper home.

‘God will bless this good Samaritan who provided me with a proper house. It is very painful that I can’t even see him, I can only hear his voice, but God will bless him," said Gumede.

Ramlutchman challenged other businessmen and people of influence to assist the needy.

"We will continue to help the poorest of the poor as long as we have the ability to do so," said the businessman." In life, we must be driven by our inner spirit, which is drawn by our faith that we have in our master, which says we must serve, and we must do it with compassion," he added.
