Radio Citizen journalist Robin Njogu has finally been laid to rest at his Sibanga farm in Trans-Nzoia county.

His final send-off was attended by hundreds of mourners, friends, family and colleagues from the Royal Media Services where he worked.

Njogu was mourned as a professional man who was a pillar in the media industry.

"We have lost a colleague, a professional, and a pillar. Njogu portrayed professionalism, respect, and networks which are important in the media industry," the head of media development and strategy at the Media Council of Kenya (MCK), Victor Bwire, said.

Njogu's father, George, said his son's death had dealt a big blow to his family and left a big gap.

"He was a gift from God, and his death is a big blow to my family. I accept that God has taken him away, but he has left a big gap," George mourned.

Njogu's widow, Carol Njogu, on the other hand, said she had lost a caring and loving husband.

Politicians who were present, including lawmakers Joshua Kutuny, Chris Wamalwa and Caleb Kositany, were barred from addressing mourners.
