Tassia tragedy: Families of missing victims cling to hope as death toll hits 10

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Monday, March 4, 2024

- Authorities say drilling and digging through the rubble must be done with care to avoid hurting those who are still trapped but alive

- Three bodies, two children and a man, were recovered on Sunday thus raising the death toll to at least 10 people

- It was alleged the building which collapsed of Friday was poorly constructed thus affecting its structural integrity

- Seven people are unaccounted for even as their families continue to camp around the site waiting to set eyes on them

The multi agency team coordinating rescue of people trapped inside a building that collapsed in Embakasi constituency on Sunday, December 8, retrieved bodies of three people, bringing the death toll to 10.

Area OCPD David Apima revealed that those who were retrieved following the Friday, December 6, incident are two children and a man.

According to the Kenya Red Cross, 36 people have since been rescued from the rubble but some seven people are still unaccounted for.

Embakasi assistant county commissioner, James Wanyoike, said the operation is slow paced since rescuers believe there are people who are still alive under the wreck and thus the need for extra caution during drilling and digging.

Wanyoike expressed optimism that the recovery exercise and clearing of the site will be completed by the end of Monday, December 9.

"Hopefully, if we are not interrupted by the rains, by the end of tomorrow we might be done. We have to clear the site. We cannot leave the debris lying around," said Wanyoike.

Among people affected is Venny Gachomba who since the building came tumbling down has not set her eyes on her two sons.

"My two boys (11 and six-year-old) left the house at around 8am and told me they were visiting a relative at the ill-fated house. I allowed them to go but told them to remember to come and take tea. I then went back to sleep.I was, however, woken up by loud noises. When I dashed outside, I was informed that a story building they had visited had collapsed...I just want them to be found alive even if they will be having fractures," said Gachomba amid sobs.

The rescue operation is being coordinated by soldiers from the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) National Disaster Management Unit (KDMU) and Kenya Red Cross.
