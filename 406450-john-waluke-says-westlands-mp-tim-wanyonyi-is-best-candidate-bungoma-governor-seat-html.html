John Waluke Says Westlands MP Tim Wanyonyi is the Best Candidate for Bungoma Governor Seat

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Saturday, February 17, 2024

- Sirisia Member of Parliament John Waluke said he had decided to shelve his 2022 bid and support Westland's MP Tim Wanyonyi for the Bungoma governorship

- Waluke vouched for Tim adding the Westlands MP is development courteous and he is the best fit for Bungoma County top seat

- The MP called on all Bungoma residents to rally behind Tim Wanyonyi in the coming General Election

Sirisia MP John Waluke has bowed out of the 2022 Bungoma gubernatorial race to back his Westlands counterpart Tim Wanyonyi.

Waluke said he had not prepared well for the race thus choosing to put aside his ambitions to support Wanyonyi.

The former army man said Wanyonyi has had an immaculate development record while serving as the Westlands MP hence he befits to be the third governor of Bungoma County.

"Many have asked me of my ambitions of being the Bungoma governor but time having gone, I have decided to step down in favour of Tim Wanyonyi, the Mp for Westlands," Waluke told journalists at his Sirisia home in Bungoma.

Waluke who was recently implicated in a multi-million NCPB scandal said the Westlands MP was a development-oriented person and he has the capacity to transform the county once he takes over from Wycliffe Wangamati, the current governor.

"I know him, he is a development courteous person. He can develop our county. I have consulted widely with other leaders in the county and we have decided to settle on him," waluke said."I urge all the people in the county, the churches, the political wing, teachers among others to support Tim Wanyonyi. Let us rally behind him. Given your support, he will transform this county," the MP added.

Being a known ardent supporter of Deputy President William Ruto, Waluke was not clear on whether or not he will ditch the DP's side given Wanyonyi is a member of the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party.

Tim Wanyonyi had earlier been endorsed by ODM leader Raila Odinga to run for the Nairobi County governorship in the coming 2022 vote.

Meanwhile, Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa and staunch DP Ruto ally has announced to have taken a 4-month period sabbatical from national politics.

The vocal MP said he will use his break to calculate the next political move he will be making after the period.

While Barasa maintained that he still supports the deputy president, many sensed he could be making his way out of Ruto's camp.

The MP hinted that he will be focusing on western Kenya politics as he seeks to draw the region closer to the 'national cake' table.

He said the Luhya community had not benefitted from its big numbers despite being one of the vote-rich tribes in Kenya.

Barasa also said he will be focusing on the development of his constituency and as well as shaping the future of the larger Bungoma county.
