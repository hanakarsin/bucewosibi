Ruto's allies arrested in Bungoma released on KSh 50k cash bail each

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Friday, March 1, 2024

- Didmus Barasa (Kimilili), Nelson Koech (Belgut), Wilson Kogo (Chesumei) and Nandi Senator Samson Cherargei were arrested during the Kabuchai by-election

- They were arraigned on Friday, March 5, and charged with possessing crude weapons and refusing to have their fingerprints taken

- They denied the charges and accused the government of political witch-hunt

Four MPs arrested in Bungoma during the Kabuchai by-election have been released on a cash bail of KSh 50 000 each.

Didmus Barasa (Kimilili), Nelson Koech (Belgut), Wilson Kogo (Chesumei) and Nandi Senator Samson Cherargei were arraigned at Bungoma Law Courts on Friday, March 5, where they were charged with possessing weapons during the mini poll and refusing to have their fingerprints taken after the arrest.

Political witch-hunt

The four MPs, who are allied to Deputy President William Ruto and the newly-formed UDA party, denied the charges and claimed the state was targeting them for political reasons.

Addressing the press shortly after being released, the lawmakers said they spent the night behind bars without being told the charges they were facing.

Barasa dismissed claims of carrying weapons and intimidating voters, arguing they were just agents supervising the voting exercise peacefully.

"This is purely politics, nothing like carrying crude weapons or threatening and intimidating voters, we were just doing our work as party agents," said the Kimili lawmaker.

Chaotic scenes

Kabuchai witnessed chaotic scenes on the voting day that saw a host of MPs supporting UDA candidate Evans Kakai roughed up with rowdy youths.

Fred Kapondi (Mt Elgon), John Waluke (Sirisia) and Millicent Omanga (Nominated Senator) were forced out of Busakala polling station by charged youths who accused them of voter bribery.

During the melee, the UDA candidate's car (a pickup) was vandalised and badly damaged by youths opposed to his candidature.

FORD Kenya candidate Majimbo Kalasinga was declared the winner by the electoral body after garnering 19 274 votes against Kakai's is 6,455 votes.
