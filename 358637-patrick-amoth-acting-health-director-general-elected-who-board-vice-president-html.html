Patrick Amoth: Acting health director general elected WHO board vice president

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Monday, July 1, 2024

-The board is composed of 34 individuals designated by the member states making the World Health Organisation

- The board holds meetings at least twice a year both in January and May immediately after the health assembly

- Amoth holds a medical doctor with a Master of Medicine degree in obstetrics/gynaecology from the University of Nairobi

The World Health Organisation (WHO) has formed a new executive board after it held election which saw about 10 countries get special seats.

Kenya's acting director general of Health Patrick Amoth was voted for the position of the vice president of the new board in an election that saw India get the presidency.

Though the election was conducted earlier on the week, the Ministry of Health confirmed the development on Friday, May 22.

"Acting Director-General of Health Patrick Amoth unanimously elected vice president of the Executive Board of World Health Organisation. Congratulations," the ministry said.

As a top decision-making organ of the global health body, the executive board is composed of 34 individuals technically qualified in the field of health.

Each one designated by a member state elected to do so by the World Health Assembly for a three-year term.

The board holds meetings at least twice a year with the first one coming up in January while the other is convened in May immediately after the health assembly.

Some of the key functions that Amoth's new team will handle include but not limited implementing decisions and policies of the assembly, advise it and to facilitate its work.

Amoth holds a medical doctor with a Master of Medicine degree in obstetrics/gynaecology from the University of Nairobi.

He served in various capacities starting from district medical services officer for Kiambu East and West districts from 2001 to 2011.

He then became senior medical specialist obstetrics/gynaecology Kiambu district hospital until March 2012.

He was then moved to Machakos Level Five Hospital in the post of chief medical specialist which he held until October 2013 when he got posted to Mama Lucy Kibaki Hospital in the same capacity and later senior deputy director of medical services.

In the wake of coronavirus in Kenya, Amoth made his first public appearance for media briefings alongside Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe.

During the pressers, his work is limited to answering technical questions pertaining to the virus spread.
