Edwin Sifuna: We're Going to Put Ring on Jubilee's Finger Because They're Equal Partner

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Tuesday, June 25, 2024
  • ODM's Edwin Sifuna said the opposition party had learnt from its mistakes in NASA and was focused on getting into an alliance with an equal party
  • Sifuna said since ODM and Jubilee were on the same level, it was the perfect choice for a coalition partner
  • He insisted there will be only two principals in the alliance, the party leaders of the outfits

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) secretary-general Edwin Sifuna has defended the outfit's coalition talks with Jubilee Party saying it will add more value to the Opposition party.

The ODM party and ruling Jubilee kicked off 14-day coalition talks meant to culminate into an alliance between the two political parties ahead of the 2022 General Election.

Speaking during an interview with Citizen TV, Sifuna noted the ruling party is an equal partner to the opposition party and was thus the right outfit to enter into a coalition with.

"When you go into a marriage, you must conduct some background checks and we have checked and can attest that there is no ring in Jubilee's finger. We are the ones who will put the ring there," he said.

Past mistakes

Citing the ODM's coalition with the three other parties that gave birth to the now-moribund National Super Alliance (NASA) coalition, Sifuna said ODM was getting into an alliance with an equal partner because it had learnt from its past mistakes.

He insisted there will be only two principals, the party leaders of the outfits.

"We have to learn from our past mistakes. Part of the problem that we experienced in the NASA coalition was that the differences between the parties were huge.You look at a party like Ford Kenya which has one senator and they want to talk and be taken like parties with 20 senators. That power dynamic will be resolved by being in a coalition with someone who is an actual equal," he said.

Jubilee-ODM coalition

As earlier reported, Jubilee Party secretary-general Raphael Tuju confirmed Jubilee and ODM were in talks and will release the commitment report in due time.

According to Tuju, the two parties will field one presidential candidate in the 2022 General Election.

Speaking on Friday, June 18, Tuju noted that the two parties are working together for the interest of the members of the public and help to unit Kenyans.

At the same time, the Jubilee leader also stated that they are open to any other political party that is interested to join.
