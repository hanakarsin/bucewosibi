New details emerge how NTSA, police refused to act after concerned motorist reported killer matunda

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Sunday, May 5, 2024

- A concerned motorists reported the Matunda bus firm to both NTSA and traffic police after recording one of its buses being driven dangerously and carelessly

- 38 people lost their lives on the morning of Sunday, December 31, 2017 after a matundu bus collided with a Lorry at Migaa on Nakuru-Eldoret Highway

- NTSA is on the spot for doing little to avert increased road accidents only resorting to knee-jerk-reactions after tragedies

New details have emerged badly exposing how the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) and the Traffic Police ignored signs of impending danger caused by Matunda buses.

In a shocking revelation by a regular motorist who talked to the Standard, said he had made reports to NTSA and traffic police stationed along the busy Northern corridor about how dangerously and carelessly the buses belonging to the Matunda Sacco were being driven.

Fearing for the worst and being a concerned motorist, Sammy Maina had presented video evidence to NTSA but all he got to date was the automatic response of: "give us your name and email address, we shall get back to you."

Shockingly, Maina made the report in September 2017, four months before a bus belonging to the Matunda sacco killed 38 people after the driver lost its control.

In his account, he had noticed one of the buses being driven dangerously and this had alarmed him.

Saddened by the tragic accident, Main took to his social media to narrate how his efforts to try and save a life were ignored by NTSA and the police.

And following the accident, NTSA jointly with the police, announced that all night travel by long distance public service vehicles had been suspended.

They directed that all travel be scheduled between 6am and 7pm but this directive has been criticised by many Kenyans who have accused the agency of being moribund and calling for its disbandment.
