World's only known white giraffe fitted with collar to protect it from poachers

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Saturday, July 13, 2024

- The rare male giraffe was collared on November 8, 2020, at the request of Ishaqbini Hirola Community Conservancy to KWS

- This was in n effort to save the species from extension after two others were killed by poachers in March 2020

- The GPRS tracker will be sending hourly updates on the giraffe's location to the rangers wh will be monitoring its movements

- Other than the giraffe, the conservationists also fitted the GPRS tracker on five endangered Hirola antelope

Seven months after poachers killed a rare white female giraffe and her seven-month-old calf at the Ishaqbini Hirola Community Conservancy in Ijara, Garissa County, the only remaining white giraffe has been collard to protect it.

The exercise to put the tracking device which was done on November 8, 2020, at the request of the conservancy board to Kenya Wildlife Service (KWS) was in an effort to save the species fro extension.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Senior Communications Officer Duncan Ndotono said the conservancy said the GPRS tracker was placed on the giraffe's horn and it will be sending hourly updates of the its location so rangers can monitor it daily.

"And in doing so keep the unique animal safe from poachers," he said.

The conservancy Manager Ahmed Noor thanked conservationists for the exercise noting the giraffe's grazing range had also been blessed with good rains in the recent past and the abundant vegetation bodes well for the future of the giraffe.

The white male is the last in a family of three. It was spotted in June, about 111 days after the other two were killed by poachers.

The giraffe has a rare genetic trait identified as leucism which results in partial loss of pigmentation in animals and causes white, pale, or patchy colouration of the skin.

The collars send a GPS position twice daily to the Sanctuary management team and this will enable rangers to remotely monitor the wildlife.

The conservancy said the process to collar the giraffe was supported by the Northern Rangelands Trust and Save Giraffes Now.
